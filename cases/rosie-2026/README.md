# Case: Rosie — Personalized mRNA Cancer Vaccine (2025-2026)

## Important Notice

This case summary is compiled from publicly available press reporting (March 2026). It has **not been peer-reviewed or published in a veterinary journal**. Details are included here to document the reported pipeline and outcomes for educational purposes. Specific clinical data (immune correlates, survival curves, detailed pathology) are not publicly available.

**Do not treat this as a validated clinical protocol.** Any attempt to replicate this approach requires veterinary oncologist oversight, institutional ethics approval, and specialist collaboration.

## What Is Publicly Known

**Patient:** Rosie, a rescue dog diagnosed with aggressive mast cell cancer.

**Owner:** Paul Conyngham, an Australian tech entrepreneur with 17 years of machine learning experience.

**Timeline:** 2025-2026 (vaccine design and administration).

**Collaborators:**
- Prof. Pall Thordarson, UNSW RNA Institute (nanomedicine/mRNA synthesis)
- UNSW Ramaciotti Centre for Genomics (sequencing)

**AI Tools Used (as reported):**
- ChatGPT: Strategy brainstorming, pipeline planning, literature synthesis
- AlphaFold (DeepMind): 3D protein structure prediction for neoantigen candidate evaluation
- Grok: Final vaccine construct refinement
- Gemini: Additional analysis

**Pipeline (as described in press):**
1. Professional tumor and normal tissue DNA sequencing at UNSW
2. AI-assisted identification of tumor-specific mutations (neoantigens)
3. AI-assisted 3D protein modeling to evaluate neoantigen targets
4. mRNA vaccine construct design encoding selected neoantigens
5. Lipid nanoparticle (LNP) formulation for delivery
6. Administration under veterinary supervision

**Reported Outcome:** Tumor shrinkage reported at 50-75%. Dog returned to normal activity. Described as the first personalized mRNA cancer vaccine for a dog.
